Micromort and Covid-19

×

In Italy, as in other Western nations, people thought they were living the worst global tragedy of the century. Of course, the military tanks that took the coffins out of Bergamo squeezed the hearts of the whole world, as did the mass graves made at the main cemetery in New York. The air and atmosphere was of war, fear, hunger, poverty, practically everywhere.

The West is privileged in that it is far removed from war-torn realities where counting hundreds of victims a day is considered 'normal'. In such countries, the pandemic is nothing more than a problem added to many others. In these realities, the economy was already destroyed long before the coronavirus arrived and the price of death is close to zero.

For this reason, Micromort did not initially set out to calculate the price of death again following the pandemic, but preferred to compare it with the symbols of Covid-19. Surgical masks, gloves, sanitizers, swabs, water and soap are just some of the symbolic elements of 2020: each of them has become indispensable to protect oneself from the coronavirus, but on the other hand, each of them also has a price - more or less high - which varies from nation to nation. In Italy, for example, surgical masks are priced at €0.60. The price of the Italian Micromort is currently € 42.65. Thus, death, and with it life, is worth 71 times a surgical mask in Italy.

But is this difference always so great in unfortunate countries?
